Non-homologous isofunctional enzyme candidates in neurologically relevant helminths

Databiomics public research resource for AnEnPi-derived NISE candidates, pathogen-specific EC hypotheses, FASTA links and downloadable tables.

Explore tablesRepository

Abstract

Neurologically relevant helminth infections remain difficult to treat safely because parasite death in sensitive tissues may intensify inflammation. This resource applies an AnEnPi-derived comparative enzymology framework to identify non-homologous isofunctional enzyme candidates, parasite-human analogs, pathogen-specific EC candidates for molecular diagnosis, and anti-migration or cycle-interruption hypotheses that may reduce neuroinvasion risk rather than simply maximize rapid parasiticidal pressure.

Workflow

Open workflow SVG

Strategy

Diagnostic candidates
ECs specific to one parasite among the seven analyzed datasets are treated as molecular diagnostic hypotheses.
Therapeutic candidates
Intergenomic parasite-human analogs are ranked with druggability, essentiality and sequence support.
Anti-migration hypotheses
Targets affecting host-interface remodeling, motility, energy homeostasis or developmental transition are separated from rapid parasiticidal hypotheses.
